Nestled within the towering embrace of an ancient oak tree, **Oakhollow** is a village that thrives in the heartwood of nature itself. Its great, hollowed trunk serves as the foundation of the settlement, with winding staircases carved into the bark, leading to snug burrows, leafy balconies, and suspended bridges woven from vines and braided reeds. Lanterns filled with fireflies glow warmly along the pathways, casting soft golden light against the tree’s bark as the village hums with quiet, everyday life.

The **[[frogfolk]]** of Oakhollow are well-adapted to their arboreal home, leaping between platforms and lily-pad-shaped landings with ease. They often live in the damp, mossy hollows and along the wooden aqueducts that carry fresh water from the [[Long River]] up into the village. Many are skilled herbalists and brewers, gathering rare fungi and medicinal plants from the shaded roots of the great oak.

The **[[squirrelfolk]]**, on the other hand, have made their homes high among the branches, constructing woven nests and burrows tucked into the tree’s massive limbs. Agile and industrious, they operate pulley systems that lift goods from the forest floor, ferrying supplies between traders and artisans with acrobatic ease. Many are skilled woodworkers, crafting intricate carvings and fine wooden tools from the ancient heartwood of their home.

At the base of the tree, **the [[#Root Market]]** bustles with life. Stalls are built from twisted roots, their canopies formed from broad oak leaves. Vendors sell everything from fresh riverfish and swamp-grown herbs to delicately carved charms said to ward off the lingering spirits of the [[Calamity Graveyard]]. [[Frogfolk]] ferrymen offer rides along the Long River in small coracle boats, guiding travelers through the winding waterways that weave through the marshlands beyond.

Despite its peaceful atmosphere, Oakhollow is no stranger to the eerie presence of the [[Calamity Graveyard]], which lies just beyond the misty riverbanks. The villagers speak in hushed tones about strange lights flickering in the distance, the quiet rustling of unseen creatures moving through the reeds at night.

# Locations
## The Swaying Bough
A rustic and welcoming inn at the heart of Oakhollow, built into the trunk of a massive oak. Its walls are adorned with carved wooden reliefs telling old Valley tales, and a great stone hearth crackles with warmth year-round. Travelers stop in for acorn bread, mushroom stew, and a mug of sweet root ale, while locals gather to trade stories beneath the glow of fireflies in hanging lanterns.
Innkeeper - [[Hedgehogs|Hedgehog]]
Rates:
**Common Room Cot** – 3 silver per night (cozy but shared with other travelers)
**Simple Burrow** – 8 silver per night (a snug, earthen room with a straw-stuffed mattress)
**Traveler’s Nook** – 1 gold per night (private, with a sturdy wooden bed and a small writing desk)

Breakfast (fresh bread, roasted nuts, and berry preserves) is included with every stay.
