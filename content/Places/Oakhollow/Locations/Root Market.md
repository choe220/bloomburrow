The Root Market coils through the lowest hollows of the great oak, where its roots break the soil and plunge into the riverbank like the fingers of a buried giant. Platforms of woven reed and polished bark are lashed directly to living roots, forming a bazaar that smells of damp earth, sap, and river mist.

Here, the oak's roots serve as walls, ceilings, and counters. Lantern-fungi glow in blues and ambers, fed with careful cuts of sap. Barges tie off along root-docks at all hours, unloaded crates of dried reeds, marsh-spices, and sealed berry casks stamped with the vigil of the [[Corpsewardens]].
