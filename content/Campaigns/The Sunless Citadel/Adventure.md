# The Sunless Citadel - A Bloomburrow Adventure

**For the DM:** This adventure is an adaptation of "The Sunless Citadel" for a **level 5 party**. It is designed to begin immediately after the party's fight with the necromancer [[Thorne Hollow]] in the [[Calamity Graveyard]].

## Part 1: The Lingering Shadow

The adventure begins in the moments after [[Thorne Hollow]] has been defeated. The air in the [[Calamity Graveyard]] is still heavy with the scent of dark magic and disturbed earth. As the party catches their breath, they can examine the items they found in the necromancer's camp.

Among the scattered pages of crude parchment, one item in particular draws the eye: an ink-stained scrap of parchment with a ritual diagram.

> A crude sketch depicts a massive, hollowed-out tree stump, its roots twisted and exposed, resembling jagged standing stones. Arcane symbols, similar to those used in Thorne Hollow's failed ritual, circle the drawing. Beneath it, a desperate scrawl reads: "Where the stars forgot to shine, the path is marked by bone and root. The Sleeper waits beyond the gate."

As the party examines the drawing, a sudden gust of wind, colder than the rest, rustles through the graveyard. It carries with it a faint, cloyingly sweet scent, like overripe fruit and freshly dug graves. The scent seems to be coming from the direction of the **Brambles**, a notoriously overgrown and dangerous region bordering the graveyard.

If the party has a ranger, druid, or any character with a connection to nature, they can make a DC 15 Wisdom (Nature) check. On a success, they recognize the scent as unnatural, a perversion of normal decay. It's a smell of something that is both alive and dead, and it speaks of a deep-seated corruption.

This note and the strange scent are the primary hooks leading the party to the **Hollow Stump**. Thorne Hollow was a lesser acolyte of a more powerful master, and this map points the way to his master's lair.

## Part 2: The Brambles and the Hollow Stump

The scent leads the party to the edge of the Brambles, a notoriously overgrown and dangerous region bordering the Calamity Graveyard. The journey through the Brambles is slow and arduous. Twisted, thorny vines as thick as a mousefolk's arm choke the path, and the air is thick with the sweet, rotten smell of the Gulthias Tree. The area is unnaturally quiet.

**Wilderness Encounter:** As the party travels through the Brambles, they are ambushed by **4 Blighted Twig Blights** and **2 Vine Blights**. These creatures are drawn to the life force of living beings and are servants of Belak. They fight until destroyed.

After an hour of difficult travel, they find the source of the smell: the **Hollow Stump**.

> A colossal, petrified tree stump, wider than any living tree in Valley, juts from the earth like a broken tooth. Its bark is a sickly gray, and the air around it is thick with the same sweet, rotten smell. The ground around the stump is sunken, forming a kind of crater. A wide, dark opening, like a wound in the petrified wood, gapes in its side, leading into the darkness within. A sturdy, knotted rope, left by a previous ill-fated adventuring party, is tied to a leaning, root-like pillar of stone and hangs down into the crater.

## Hollow Stump: Fortress Level

### General Features
*   **Walls:** The "fortress" is the hollowed-out interior of the petrified great tree. The walls are made of a stone-like, hardened wood, cold and damp to the touch.
*   **Light:** Beyond the entrance, the stump is lightless. Descriptions assume the characters have a light source.
*   **Ventilation:** The air is stale but breathable, renewed by small fissures in the petrified wood that lead to the surface.
*   **Debris:** The ground is covered in a treacherous layer of petrified wood splinters, dust, and the remains of the stump's former living structure. This is **difficult terrain** unless otherwise noted.

### 1. Ledge

> A sandy ledge overlooks a subterranean gulf of darkness to the west. The ledge is wide but rough. Sand, rocky debris, and the bones of small animals cover it. A rough-hewn stairwell, carved into the petrified wood, zigs and zags down the side of the ledge, descending into darkness.

The far wall of the chasm is 250 feet to the west, and the bottom of the subterranean vault is 80 feet below.

*   **Creatures:** Drawn by the occasional animal that accidentally falls into the ravine, **4 Giant Rats** and **1 Diseased Giant Rat** (use stats for a Dire Wolf with a disease-carrying bite) lurk in the rubble. They are followers of Guthash, the "Mama Rat" in Area 30, and will try to ambush the party.
*   **Investigation:** A DC 15 Wisdom (Survival) check reveals humanoid footprints (from the previous party) and rat tracks of unusual size. Among the rubble is an old, ash-filled fire pit containing a few rough-hewn Ratfolk spear tips.

---

### 2. Switchback Stairs

The 5-foot-wide stairs carved into the petrified wood are rough but stable. As the party descends, they see the "fortress" proper.

> A fortress emerges from the darkness. The subterranean citadel, though impressive, seems long forgotten, if the lightless windows, cracked crenellations, and leaning towers are any indication. All is quiet, though a cold breeze blows up from below, bringing with it the scent of dust and a faint trace of rot.

### 3. Crumbled Courtyard

> The narrow stairs empty into a small courtyard, apparently the top of what was once a crenellated battlement. The buried citadel has sunk so far into the earth that the battlement is now level with the surrounding floor. That floor stretches away to the north and south, composed of a layer of treacherous, crumbled masonry, which reaches to an unknown depth. To the west looms the surviving structure of what must be the Sunless Citadel. A tower stands on the west side of the courtyard.

*   **Debris Field:** The crumbled masonry surrounding the courtyard is difficult terrain. A creature moving across it must make a DC 12 Dexterity (Acrobatics) check for every 15 feet traversed. On a failed check, the creature is unable to move. If the check fails by 5 or more, a slab of masonry shifts, and the character falls into a 10-foot-deep cavity filled with sharp debris (taking 1d6 piercing damage). Climbing out requires a DC 12 Strength (Athletics) check.
*   **Hidden Pit (Level 5 version):** A concealed, 20-foot-deep pit is marked on the map. It's covered by a pressure-sensitive trapdoor made of petrified wood.
    *   **Detection:** DC 17 Wisdom (Perception) to spot the seams of the trapdoor.
    *   **Mechanism:** When 50 lbs or more of weight is placed on it, the trapdoor springs open. A mechanism resets the trap 1 minute later.
    *   **Disarming:** A character can use thieves' tools to jam the lid shut with a successful DC 17 Dexterity check. Disabling the reset mechanism (in the pit wall) is a DC 20 Dexterity check with thieves' tools.
*   **Creatures in Pit:** The pit contains the skeletal remains of two Ratfolk, one recently dead Ratfolk, and **2 Swarms of Rats** that attack any who fall in.
*   **Treasure:** The fresh Ratfolk corpse has a scimitar, a shield, a belt pouch containing 35 sp and 12 gp, and a crude drawing of a Skunkfolk with a knife stuck through it.

### 4. Tower Shell

> This circular area is cobbled with cracked granite, upon which sprawl the bodies of four Ratfolk, apparently slain in combat. One corpse stands with its back against the western wall, the spear that killed it still skewering it and holding it upright. Three wooden doors lead from this area. A hollow tower of loose masonry reaches thirty feet into the air, but the intervening floors and stairs are gone, except for a couple of crumbled ledges.

*   **Bodies:** The four Ratfolk have been dead for over a week, killed by the resident Skunkfolk. If the spear is removed from the pinned Ratfolk, its body slumps to reveal Draconic runes on the wall behind it: "Ashardalon." This hints at the original dragon-cult builders.
*   **Secret Door:** A trapped secret door leads to area 5.
    *   **Detection:** DC 20 Wisdom (Perception) check to find.
    *   **Mechanism:** The door opens via a masonry block that acts as a lever.
*   **Spore-Coated Needle Trap (Level 5 version):** If the lever is pulled incorrectly, a needle coated in toxic spores shoots out, dealing 1 piercing damage and forcing the target to make a DC 14 Constitution saving throw or take 2d10 poison damage and be poisoned for 1 hour.
    *   **Disarming:** Disabling the needle requires thieves' tools and a successful DC 17 Dexterity check. If the check fails by 5 or a more, the trap is triggered.

### 5. Secret Pocket

> This pocket chamber is damp and cold. The skeletons of three long-dead archers slump against rubble-filled arrow slits along the east and south wall.

The skeletons date back to the time before the stump was petrified. That calamity killed all three archers, at the same time instilling in them the curse of undeath as servants of the blight.

*   **Creatures:** If anyone enters this chamber, **3 Bonebound Archers** animate. Use **Skeleton** stats, but they are animalfolk skeletons (rabbits, mice, etc.) animated by blight.
    *   **Level 5 Scaling:** Their shortbow attacks deal an additional 1d6 poison damage on a hit, a gift from the Gulthias Tree's influence that permeates the stump.
*   **Treasure:** Each skeleton has 1d10 gp and one ***+1 Arrow*** in a special socket on its quiver. Because the skeletons are mindless, they don't use the magical arrows.

### 6. Old Approach

> The petrified wood walls of this twenty-foot-wide hall are in poor repair. The far end has collapsed, filling the southern section with rubble. The western wall is in much better shape than the other walls, and it holds a stone door with a rearing dragon carved in relief on it. The door has a single keyhole, situated in the rearing dragon's open mouth.

*   **Dragon Door:** This door has a mechanical lock and a powerful *arcane lock* spell cast on it.
    *   **Level 5 Scaling:** The Strength (Athletics) check to force the door is DC 30. The Dexterity check to bypass the magical lock using thieves' tools is DC 25. A *knock* spell suppresses the magical lock for 10 minutes, lowering the DCs by 10. The mechanical lock remains. The key, held by the Skunkfolk leader in Area 21, bypasses all locks.
*   **Creature:** One **Diseased Giant Rat** (as Area 1) hides in the rubble and attacks anyone who moves within 5 feet of it.

### 7. Gallery of Forlorn Notes

> As the door opens, a hissing noise and a puff of dust around the door indicate that the chamber beyond has been sealed for ages. Dust, long undisturbed, covers every surface in this large gallery. The air here is stale. Three alcoves are on the north wall, and one is on the south wall. Each alcove contains a dust-covered stone pedestal with a fist-sized crystalline globe resting on it. The globes in the northern alcoves are cracked and dark, but the globe in the southern alcove glows with a soft blue light. Faint tinkling notes issue from it.

*   **Music Globe:** If a character moves within 5 feet of the lit globe, brooding music begins to play throughout the area.
    *   **Level 5 Scaling:** Any creature that can hear the music must make a DC 16 Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save, a creature becomes charmed. While charmed, it can only take the Dash action to move toward the Courtyard (Area 3), driven by a magical despair to flee the stump. The effect ends once the creature reaches the courtyard. The effect can be ended with a *dispel magic* (DC 16) or by destroying the globe.
*   **The Globe:** The globe has AC 12 and 15 hit points. If attacked, the music grows louder, and the save DC increases to 18. If destroyed, it shatters and the music stops.

### 8. Pressure Plate

> The air is stale in this twenty-foot-long corridor, which leads to another closed stone door.

*   **Spore Dart Trap (Level 5 version):** The central 10-foot-square section of the corridor is a pressure plate. When activated, a volley of four darts fires from tiny holes in the ceiling. Make a separate attack roll for each dart against the triggering creature: +8 to hit, 2 (1d4) piercing damage plus 5 (1d10) poison damage.
    *   **Detection & Disarming:** DC 18 Intelligence (Investigation) to spot the pressure plate. DC 18 Dexterity with thieves' tools to disable.

### 9. Dragon Riddle

> Dust fills this hall like a layer of gray snow. In the rounded northern end of the chamber stands a ten-foot-tall sculpture of a coiled dragon carved from red-veined white marble.

*   **Dragon Sculpture:** When a creature moves within 5 feet, a *magic mouth* spell speaks a riddle in Common:
    > *We come at night without being fetched; we disappear by day without being stolen. What are we?*
    > (Answer: stars)
*   **Secret Door:** A secret door in the western wall (DC 20 Wisdom (Perception) to find) opens only when the riddle's answer is spoken aloud. It closes on its own after 1 minute.

### 10. Honor Guard

> Dust cloaks the contents of this twenty-foot-wide hall. Six alcoves line the walls, three to the north and three to the south. Each alcove except the southwest one holds a humanoid figure carved of petrified wood, resembling ancient animalfolk heroes in ceremonial armor. A stone archway at the west end of the hall opens into a wide room. A dark pit is situated before the archway.

*   **Spiked Pit:** The pit is 15 feet deep. Its bottom is filled with sharpened roots that deal 11 (2d10) piercing damage to a creature that falls in, plus falling damage. The walls are rough (DC 12 Strength (Athletics) to climb).
*   **Creature:** A **Corrupted Pixie** named Jot has been twisted by Belak's influence. It was bound here long ago to guard the tomb. It is invisible and hiding near the archway into Area 12. It attacks the first creature to cross the pit.
    *   **Jot's Stats:** Use **Pixie** stats, but its alignment is Neutral Evil. Its arrows are coated in a mild toxin that causes confusion (as the *confusion* spell, DC 12 Wisdom save to resist, lasts 1 round) instead of putting creatures to sleep.
*   **Development:** Jot is a malicious trickster, not a fanatic. If it takes any damage, it will laugh and say, "The binding is broken! My watch is over!" It will then use its invisibility to escape and will harass the party later when they are engaged in other fights.

### 11. Secret Room

*   **Secret Door:** A secret trapdoor in the floor (DC 20 Wisdom (Perception) check to find) opens into a crawl space connecting to Area 12.

### 12. Tomb of a Failed Dragonpriest

> Violet-tinged petrified wood panels line the floor and walls, though all are cracked or broken. Sconces are attached to the walls at each corner. One holds a torch that burns with greenish *continual flame*. A marble sarcophagus, easily nine feet long, lies in the room's center. The coffin is carved with dragon imagery, and the head of the sarcophagus resembles a dragon's head. Rusting iron clasps firmly lock down the lid.

*   **Sarcophagus:** Opening the six rusted latches requires a successful DC 17 Strength check for each. Removing the lid then requires a DC 17 Strength check (or combined Strength of 40). When opened, a *sequester* spell breaks with a flash of green light.
*   **Creature:** Inside is a former **Lizardfolk Shaman**, now a horrifying, troll-like creature after performing forbidden rituals. He was entombed alive for his transgressions. He has forgotten his name and attacks on sight.
    *   **Lizardfolk Abomination:** Use **Troll** statistics, with these changes:
        *   It speaks Draconic and Elvish.
        *   **Blighted Claws:** On a hit, its claw attack deals an extra 1d6 necrotic damage.
        *   **Stench of Decay (Recharge 6):** As a bonus action, the abomination can release a foul gas in a 10-foot radius. Any creature in the area must succeed on a DC 14 Constitution saving throw or be poisoned for 1 minute.
*   **Treasure:** The abomination has an ornate ceremonial dagger (worth 125 gp). Scattered in the sarcophagus are 220 sp, 50 gp, and four *spell scrolls*: *command*, *cure wounds* (2nd level), *inflict wounds* (2nd level), and *guiding bolt* (2nd level).

### 14. Bound Elementals

> The petrified wood door sealing this chamber portrays a carving of swirling winds and water.

The door is locked (DC 20 Dexterity with thieves' tools to open).

> This ten-foot-square chamber is hewn from stone. It contains an upright keg fashioned of rusted iron. Rusted pipes lead from the keg into the floor.

The keg is magically bound and contains two elemental spirits.

*   **Creatures:** If the keg is breached (AC 15, 25 hit points), an **Ice Mephit** and a **Steam Mephit** are released. They were bound here long ago as eternal guardians. They are enraged and attack any creature they see.
*   **Treasure:** Within the keg are five tiny sapphires (worth 25 gp each) that were used in the binding ritual.

### 15. The Beetle's Pen

> Crudely painted symbols and glyphs, scribed in a pungent green dye, decorate this large and irregularly shaped crumbling chamber. A large pit in the center shows evidence of a recent fire. A metallic cage in the middle of the southern wall contains a gaping hole and stands empty. A small wooden bench draped with green cloth is next to the cage, and several small objects rest on it. A bedroll lies near the wooden bench, and the sound of whimpering comes from inside it.

This was the pen for the Skunkfolk's prized Giant Fire Beetle. The invading Ratfolk stole it a week ago.

*   **Creature:** **Slink**, a young **Skunkfolk** (use **Commoner** stats), is here. He was the "Keeper of the Beetle" and is heartbroken. He hides in his bedroll and whimpers. If disturbed, he is frightened but can be convinced to talk.
*   **Slink's Plea:** If asked about the cage or a beetle/dragon, he will wail, "The Ratfolk! They stole Iggy, our sacred beetle!" He will offer to lead the party to the Skunkfolk elder, Yarrow-Scent, promising safe passage if they agree to help rescue Iggy. The password for safe passage is "Moon-musk" (spoken in Sylvan).
*   **Investigation:** The symbols on the walls are crude warnings against thieves. A search of the cage reveals iridescent beetle shell fragments. A DC 15 Intelligence (Nature) check identifies them as belonging to a Giant Fire Beetle.
*   **Treasure:** On the bench are four tiny jade figurines of beetles (worth 20 gp each).

### 16. Skunkfolk Guardroom

If triggered, a clay pot filled with concentrated musk and rotten compost falls on the creature in the doorway. The target and all creatures within 5 feet must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or be poisoned for 10 minutes. A creature can repeat the save at the end of each of its turns. (DC 15 Dexterity with thieves' tools to disarm).
> A small pit of embers smolders in the center of this chamber. Arrayed around it are several small woven mats and bedrolls. The air is thick with musk.

*   **Creatures:** **Three Skunkfolk Warriors** (use **Guard** stats with a Stench Spray ability once per day: 15-ft cone, DC 13 Con save or be poisoned for 1 minute) stand guard. They are hostile unless the party has Slink or uses the password. If the party is friendly, they direct them to Yarrow-Scent (Area 21).

*   **Creatures:** This is the food for the Giant Fire Beetle. The Skunkfolk have neglected them, and the barrier is weak. One round after the door is opened, **2 Swarms of Insects (Centipedes)** burst out and attack, dispersing after 1 round.

### 18. Prison

> Four rat-like humanoids are shackled by thick, rusted chains to a large iron spike set in the floor. Several broken weapons and sundered shields lie in one corner.

*   **Creatures:** Four **Ratfolk** (use **Goblin** stats) are chained here. They were captured during skirmishes. They will promise anything for their freedom but will betray the party at the first opportunity. Freeing them will make the Skunkfolk hostile.

### 19. Hall of Elders

> A double row of relief-carved petrified wood columns march the length of this long, large hall. The worn carvings depict ancient Skunkfolk elders and heroes of their clan.

*   **Creatures:** A squad of **three Skunkfolk Veterans** (use **Veteran** stats) patrol the hall. They are on high alert for Ratfolk incursions. They are hostile unless the party is escorted.
*   **Development:** Combat here is extremely dangerous. It will alert all nearby Skunkfolk, including Yarrow-Scent from Area 21, who will arrive in 2 rounds.

### 20. Skunkfolk Colony

The door is barred from the inside. The password "Moon-musk" gets the party inside.

> Several small but smoky fires light this wide and high chamber. Tanning racks, baskets of roots, and other signs of a thriving community are visible through the haze. Several skunk-like animalfolk go about their business.

*   **Creatures:** Thirty Skunkfolk live here. Only **three are Skunkfolk Warriors** (as Area 16). The rest are non-combatants who will flee from any fight. They will defer all questions to their elder, Yarrow-Scent.

### 21. Throne of the Elder

> A short throne stands near the west wall, constructed of woven roots and smooth river stones, stacked against an old altar. On top of the altar sit a variety of small, natural items. The portion of the altar that serves as the throne's back features a carving of a rearing dragon. A metallic key is held firmly in the dragon's open jaws.

*   **Creatures:** **Yarrow-Scent**, the **Skunkfolk Elder** (use **Druid** stats), sits on the throne. She is guarded by **two Skunkfolk Veterans** (as Area 19). She is wary but not hostile if the party has Slink or the password.
*   **Negotiation:** Yarrow-Scent is desperate to get the clan's sacred beetle, Iggy, back from the Ratfolk. She explains that the beetle's glowing glands are a symbol of their clan's spirit. If the party agrees to rescue Iggy, she will offer them a reward: the key to the Dragon Door (Area 6) and their choice of two items from the altar. She is also willing to sell the key for 100 gp.
*   **Treasure:** The key in the dragon's mouth opens the door to Area 6 (note: this is a change from the original module's Area 7). On the altar are: a *Quaal's Feather Token (Tree)*, spell scrolls of *mage armor*, *spider climb*, and *knock*, and a flask with three doses of *Elixir of Health*. Yarrow-Scent also has a *Potion of Greater Healing*.

### 26. Dry Fountain

> Dust and bits of stony debris and rubble lie scattered on the floor. An ornate fountain is built into the eastern wall. Though cracked and stained, the fountain's overarching carving of a diving dragon retains its beauty. A relief-carved wooden door stands on the western wall.

*   **Fountain:** The fountain is dry. Under *detect magic*, it emits a conjuration aura. The inscription in Draconic reads, "Let there be fire." Speaking the phrase causes the fountain to fill with a liquid that functions as a single *Potion of Fire Breath*. This magic works only once.
*   **Western Door:** The door is carved with skeletal dragons. An inscription reads, "Rebuke the dead, open the way." The door is locked and opens only when targeted by an effect that turns undead (or a DC 25 Dexterity check with thieves' tools).
*   **Scythe Trap (Level 5 version):** Attempting to open the door without the proper key or turning effect triggers a scythe trap. A spectral blade, invisible until it strikes, attacks: +10 to hit, 3d10 force damage. Detection and disarming remain difficult (DC 20 for both).

### 27. Sanctuary

### 37. The Stolen Beetle

The door is locked (DC 15 Dexterity with thieves' tools).
> Mounted and stuffed animal heads adorn the walls. The assortment of heads includes cattle, rats, and a couple of Skunkfolk heads. Smashed cabinets and tables litter the room. Thin patches of frost coat sections of the walls and debris.

*   **Creature:** **Iggy**, the stolen **Giant Fire Beetle** is here. Enraged and frightened, it attacks any who enter.
    *   **Level 5 Scaling:** Use the statistics for a **Young White Dragon**, but its Cold Breath is re-themed as a spray of burning, caustic fluid (dealing Acid damage instead of Cold).
*   **Treasure:** Iggy has made a nest of shiny objects, including a jade figurine (20 gp), a crystal goblet (5 gp), and a sealed bone scroll case containing a fragment of lore about the lost dwarven redoubt of Khundrukar (can be sold for 100 gp).

### 40. The Gnaw-Bone Clan

> What might once have been a cathedral is now a Ratfolk lair, thick with the filth of years. Scores of wall- and floor-mounted sconces filled with violet-glowing fungi provide illumination. Dozens of rat-like humanoids go about their business.

*   **Creatures:** This is the main warren of the Gnaw-Bone clan. **Thirty-four Ratfolk** are here, but only **four are Ratfolk Warriors** (as Area 31). The rest are non-combatants and will flee.

### 41. Hall of the Rat King

> A circular shaft pierces the floor of this forty-foot-diameter domed chamber. Dim violet light shines out of the shaft, revealing sickly white and gray vines that coat its walls. A crudely fashioned throne of scrap metal and bone sits against the curve of the northwestern wall. A large iron chest serves as the throne's footstool. A sickly sapling grows in a wide stone pot next to the throne.

*   **Creatures:**
    *   **Durnn, the Rat King:** A powerful **Ratfolk Warlord** (use **Hobgoblin Warlord** stats). He wears the armor of the fallen adventurer, Talgen Hucrele.
    *   **Grenl, the Sewer-Priest:** A **Ratfolk Sewer-Priest** (use **Goblin Boss** stats but with cleric spells: *Poison Spray, Inflict Wounds, Bane*).

    *   **Pet:** A **Twig Blight**, a gift from Belak, is in the stone pot.

*   **Development:** Durnn fights to the death. He wears Talgen's gold signet ring. Grenl secretly hates Durnn and may negotiate if he falls, hoping to seize power over the Gulthias Tree herself. The party learns that Durnn slew Talgen in a rage but sent Sharwyn and Sir Braford down to Belak.
*   **Trapped Chest:** The iron chest is locked (DC 15) and has a poison needle trap (DC 15 to find, DC 15 to disarm; 1 piercing + 1d10 poison damage, DC 15 Con save for half).
*   **Treasure:** The chest contains 300 gp and two onyx gems (50 gp each). Durnn has the key. Grenl has keys to Areas 34 and 37.

## Hollow Stump: Grove Level

The Grove Level is a single, vast cavern, and the true heart of the blight. The air is damp, chilly, and redolent with the odors of loam and decay. Luminescent fungus, shedding a sickly violet light, clings to the walls and ceiling.

---
### 42. Central Garden

> Luminescent fungus sheds violet light throughout this wide cavern. The air is damp and chilly. A layer of earth, mixed with rotting vegetation and the remains of cave animals, covers the floor. Several varieties of mushrooms and fungi grow on the detritus, as well as a few sickly saplings.

*   **Creatures:** Two **Blighted Skeletons** (use **Ghoul** stats) are tending the fungus garden. In addition, **four Vine Blights** are rooted here, and will ambush any non-servant of Belak.

---
### 43. The Hunter's Abode

> The floor of this rough cavern is stained and smells of blood and animal musk. Light from glowing fungus reveals a pallet of matted furs, a wide wooden board with weapons affixed to it, and a great cloak of patchy black fur.

*   **Creature:** This is the lair of a **Blighted Owlbear**, a creature twisted by Belak's influence. It has an additional 1d6 poison damage on its beak and claw attacks. It is a savage hunter and considers anything in its territory to be prey.
*   **Treasure:** A box hidden under the furs (DC 15 Perception) contains 241 sp, 54 gp, and a *Potion of Animal Friendship*.

### 45. Rift Node

> The rift widens into a cavern-like chamber. Small, two-foot-diameter holes riddle the floor. A dim fiery glow shines out of one such hole.

*   **Creature:** A **Salamander** rests in the glowing hole, a powerful elemental servant bound by Belak. It is hostile to any who approach.
*   **Treasure:** The salamander guards two large, flawless sapphires (worth 250 gp each).

---
### 47. Belak's Laboratory

> Two rows of columns covered in luminescent fungus march the length of the hall. The floor is cracked and stained, and on it sit many small wooden tables covered in alchemical equipment and plant specimens. The many doors leading off this hall are all partly open.

*   **Creatures:** Belak's assistants, **four Ratfolk Cultists** (use **Cultist** stats) and **two Ratfolk Fanatics** (use **Cult Fanatic** stats), work here. They are all branded with the symbol of the Hollow Veil. They attack on sight.
*   **Experiments:** The side rooms contain various horrid experiments, including a giant rat being transformed into a woody monstrosity, and cages of animalfolk awaiting their turn.
*   **Treasure:** The lab contains a *Healer's Kit*, a *Herbalism Kit*, and an *Alchemist's Supplies*.

### 53. Belak's Study

The door is locked (DC 20 Dexterity with thieves' tools).
> A layer of soil covers the floor. Rough wooden shelves are filled with tomes and scrolls. A desk stands in the center. Fungus on the ceiling provides light for several small bushes and pale saplings growing in the soil.

*   **Trapped Tome:** A book titled *Treasures of the Fire Lords* has a *glyph of warding* (explosive runes, cold) on the second page. (DC 16 Investigation to find). It deals 5d8 cold damage in a 20-foot radius (DC 16 Dex save for half).
*   **Treasure:** The study contains spell scrolls of *entangle* and *protection from poison*. The desk holds 365 gp and four agate gems (20 gp each).

### 55. Twilight Grove

> Pale, spindly briars press close, casting twisted shadows on the earth floor in the violet light. The briars are difficult terrain for any creature larger than Small. A creature moving through takes 1 piercing damage for every foot moved.

*   **Creatures:** **Ten Twig Blights** are spread throughout the grove. They attack in waves (1d4 per round) as the party moves through.

### 56. The Gulthias Tree (Climax)

> A walled clearing stands in the center of the briars. Beneath the fungal light grows an evil tree. Its blackened, twisted limbs reach upward, like a skeletal hand clawing its way out of the earth. Before it stand several figures: a heavily armored man, a robed woman, and a tall Raccoonfolk in brown robes, armed with a gnarled staff and a sickle. All three have black eyes and gray skin with the texture of bark.

*   **Creatures:**
    *   **Belak the Root-Twisted:** The final villain. (See Appendix A).
    *   **Sir Braford:** A human **Gladiator**. He is a thrall of the Gulthias Tree.
    *   **Sharwyn Hucrele:** A human **Mage**. She is also a thrall.
    *   **Kulket:** Belak's **Giant Frog**, which hides in the tree.
    *   **Reinforcements:** Three **Vine Blights** and any remaining Twig Blights from Area 55.
*   **The Gulthias Tree:** The tree itself (AC 15, 100 HP, vulnerable to fire, resistant to piercing/bludgeoning, immune to poison/psychic) is the source of the corruption. Attacking it draws the ire of Belak and all blights. If the tree is destroyed, Belak loses control of his thralls, who then turn on him.
*   **Development:** Belak attempts to reason with the party, offering them a place as his "supplicants." He is arrogant and believes his cause is just. If a fight breaks out, he uses his spells to control the battlefield while his thralls and blights attack. He fights to the death to protect his precious tree.
*   **Treasure:** Belak carries a *Wand of Entangle* (10 charges, regains 1d6+4 daily), two *Potions of Greater Healing*, and the key to his study (Area 53). Sharwyn wears her family signet ring.

## Appendix B: Magic Items

### Night Caller
*Wondrous item, uncommon*
This whistle is carved from a transparent crystal. The whistle can be heard up to 600 feet away. The whistle has 3 charges. When you use an action to blow the whistle, you can expend 1 charge to cast the *animate dead* spell. The whistle regains all expended charges daily at dawn.

### Shatterspike
*Longsword, uncommon (requires attunement)*
When you hit a construct, an object, or a structure with this magic sword, the hit is a critical hit.
