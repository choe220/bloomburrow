# The Starless Gate
Two ancient stone pillars, weathered and cracked, stand on either side of a massive rock face. 
Faint carvings remain — intricate patterns of twisting vines and animalfolk in solemn poses, as if standing vigil.
Faint traces of what may have once been runes linger along the bases of the pillars, though they are now fractured and incomplete.
Between the pillars once held a grand stone door, but it now lies in ruin
Jagged edges protrude from the rock face, suggesting the door was shattered rather than simply worn away
Above the entrance, a weathered inscription reads:
>"When the stars turned away, we sealed what could not die."

## The Threshold Chamber
### Description
Flickering sconces magically reignite as the party enters, casting long shadows. Whispering echoes occasionally fill the air.
A ceremonial chamber filled with partially collapsed statues of various animalfolk in armor, 4 stand alone near the door at the opposite end:
- A proud **Mousefolk warrior**, clad in a detailed stone cuirass with a tattered cloak draped over their shoulders. Though the head has crumbled away, the warrior’s pose remains striking — one paw clutching a slender spear, the other holding a small round shield.
	Carved runes along the pedestal read:
	_"May the smallest stand against the greatest."_
- A towering **Batfolk elder**, wings partially extended, with long ceremonial robes cascading to the floor. One wing is wrapped protectively around a glowing stone orb, though the orb's light has since dimmed.
	The statue's eyes are hollow, as though once inlaid with precious gems.
	Inscribed words read:
	"*Seeker of stars, guardian of what lies beyond sight.*"
- A broad-shouldered **Lizardfolk** carved in an imposing stance, clutching a massive stone hammer. Their expression is fierce, with jagged scars etched across the stone face, immortalizing their valor.
	The base of the statue is fractured, but a fragment of its inscription remains:
	"*Strength unyielding, for the tide may break upon the stone.*"
- A **Squirrelfolk scout**, depicted mid-leap from a gnarled tree branch. Despite its damage, the statue conveys a sense of motion. A quiver of arrows and a finely detailed bow are slung across their back.
	"*No shadow moves unseen, no secret remains untouched.*"

Intelligence check of DC 10 or higher gleans that the statues all seem to be facing the next door as if protecting against it

### Puzzle
On the door to the next chamber, an inscription reads:

>"To face the darkness, the smallest must stand first.  
>The stars will watch, though they may not shine.  
>The stone will endure, unyielding.  
>And the shadows shall move last, unseen."

In front of each of the four statues is a stone pressure plate.

**Solution:** The party must step on the pressure plates in the correct order, which is **Mousefolk**, **Batfolk**, **Lizardfolk**, **Squirrelfolk**.

**Interactions & Consequences:**

*   **Correct Order:** When a plate is stepped on in the correct sequence, the eyes of the corresponding statue will emit a soft, steady glow. A low, resonant chime echoes through the chamber, and the door unlocks slightly, grinding open a few inches with each correct step. After the final correct step, the door grinds fully open.

*   **Incorrect Order:** If a plate is stepped on out of sequence, the puzzle resets. Any glowing eyes on the statues go dark, and the door, if partially open, slams shut. The statue corresponding to the incorrect plate animates and attacks the creature on the plate before instantly returning to its position.

    *   **Mousefolk Warrior:** The stone spear thrusts forward with surprising speed. The target must make a **DC 15 Dexterity saving throw**, taking 2d6 piercing damage on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one.
    *   **Batfolk Elder:** The dim orb in the statue's grasp flares with a sickly purple light. The target must make a **DC 15 Constitution saving throw**, taking 2d8 necrotic damage on a failed save as the whispers from the chamber intensify into a cacophony in their mind.
    *   **Lizardfolk:** The massive stone hammer swings down in a powerful arc. The target must make a **DC 15 Strength saving throw**, taking 2d10 bludgeoning damage on a failed save and being knocked prone.
    *   **Squirrelfolk Scout:** A volley of magically conjured stone arrows fires from the statue's bow. The target must make a **DC 15 Dexterity saving throw**, taking 3d6 piercing damage on a failed save.

**Skill Checks:**

*   **Investigation (DC 13):** Examining the pressure plates reveals that the one before the Mousefolk statue is significantly more worn than the others, suggesting it is the first to be pressed.
*   **Arcana (DC 14):** A successful check on the door's inscription reveals faint traces of abjuration magic, confirming it as a magical lock. A character who succeeds on this check by 5 or more also understands that the sequence is tied to the statues.
*   **Perception (DC 12):** A character inspecting the Batfolk statue will notice that its eyes are hollowed out, as if they once held gems. They might also notice that the stone orb it clutches is a separate piece of stone, not part of the original carving.
