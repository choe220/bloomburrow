Snakefolk are hardly seen in the lands of [[Valley]], and most snakes any [[animalfolk]] would have seen would take the form of [[Calamity Beasts]] rather than [[animalfolk]]. Still, snakefolk do live outside of [[Valley]] in their own communities.

Compared to other [[animalfolk]], snakefolk can grow to be incredibly large.

Most [[animalfolk]] are likely to fear snakefolk and see them as predators or liars. A snakefolk in [[Valley]] would likely live on the fringes of the society, or find more solace in the seedy underbelly of larger cities.

# Snakefolk Traits
**Creature Type:** Humanoid
**Size:** Medium (about 4–6 feet tall) or Small (about 1-4 feet tall), chosen when you select this species
**Speed:** 30 feet

---
As a Snakefolk, you have these special traits.

_**Bite.**_ You have sharp fangs. When you use your Unarmed Strike to deal damage with them, you can deal 1d6 plus your Strength modifier Piercing damage instead of the normal damage of your Unarmed Strike.

_**Blindsight.**_ You have Blindsight with a range of 10 feet.

_**Constrictor.**_ When you use the Grapple option of your Unarmed Strikes, the target has Disadvantage on any ability check it makes to avoid or end the Grappled condition.

_**Extract Venom.**_ When you finish a Long Rest, you can craft a vial of Basic Poison using an empty Vial as raw materials.

_**Serpentine Form.**_ You don't have hands. You can use your mouth or tail to manipulate an object, open or close a door or container, pick up or set down an object, or wield a weapon that doesn't have the Two-Handed property.

_**Swimming.**_ You have a Swim Speed equal to your Speed.

_**Venomous Strike.**_ Immediately after you hit a creature with your Bite as part of the Attack action on your turn, you can take a Bonus Action to give the target the Poisoned condition until the start of your next turn.

You can use this trait a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a Long Rest.
