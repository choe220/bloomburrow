### Step 1: The World Concept

Instead of mapping an entire planet, start with a name and a **one-sentence tone/history**.

- **Action:** Give the world a name and decide on the "vibe" (e.g., high fantasy but magic is rare).
- **Benefit:** A single historical event (like the fall of an empire) provides instant hooks for ruins, items, and ancient mysteries.

### Step 2: Starting Location (The Village)

Start small to avoid burnout. A small town is easier to manage than a metropolis.

- **Purpose:** Define why the town exists (e.g., a farming community supplying grain to a city) 
- **Population:** Estimate the number of buildings (e.g., 40–50) and residents (e.g., 200) to keep it intimate.
- **Surroundings:** Note three "layers" of geography (e.g., fields, then a forest, then mountains) to help you improvise travel

### Step 3: Points of Interest & Key Figures

Add personality with 4–5 specific locations and characters to act as "quest magnets."

- **Essential Spots:** A tavern for gossip, a forge for gear/rumors, a general store for supplies, and a temple for mystery
- **NPCs with Quirks:** Give each location a specific person with a distinct trait (e.g., a retired adventurer blacksmith or a soft-spoken priestess with secrets)

### Step 4: Adventure Hooks

Create 1–3 immediate conflicts to grab the players' attention.

- **Examples:** Smuggling through the temple, disappearing livestock in the woods, or a mysterious traveler hiding a cursed relic

### Step 5: The Local Realm

Connect the town to the nearest seat of power to imply a larger world.

- **Action:** Name the local Lord, their keep (about a day away), and the Capital City (2–3 days away)
- **Conflict:** Introduce local tension, like resentment over high taxes demanded by the Lord

### Step 6: Factions & The Greater Kingdom

Define 3–4 powerful groups that influence the land.

- **Core Groups:** The video suggests a **Royal Court** (politics), **Merchants Guild** (economy), **Arcane Remnant** (mystery), and **Military Vanguard** (security)
- **Interconnection:** Think about how these groups might be at odds, such as the Merchants Guild pushing for control over the village’s resources

### Step 7: The World Beyond (Global Scale)

Give the world "breath" by naming distant lands without fully detailing them.

- **Action:** Name 3–4 neighboring regions (e.g., an icy north, a pirate-filled coast) so you have an answer when players ask "What's across the sea?"
- **Global Threat:** Establish a massive event that serves as a backdrop, like an Intercontinental War, to increase the stakes
