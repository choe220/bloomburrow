The government of [[Valley]] is a two-tiered system, with local lords governing their own settlements and a council of elders providing wider oversight.

# The Valley Council

[[Valley]] is governed by the [[Valley Council]], a council of elders composed of representatives from each of the major settlements. The council meets in [[Three-Tree City]] to discuss matters that affect the entire [[Valley]], such as defense, trade agreements, and responding to major threats like the [[Calamity Beasts]].

The member settlements of the [[Valley Council]] are:
- [[Fountainport]]
- [[Oakhollow Village]]
- [[Cactus Point Village]]
- [[Goodhill]]
- [[Rockface Village]]
- [[Lupinflower Village]]

# Local Government

Each settlement in [[Valley]] is governed by a local lord, who is responsible for the day-to-day administration of their village or city. These lords also represent their settlement on the [[Valley Council]].

# Historical Context
While [[Valley]] is now governed by the [[Valley Council|Council]] and local lords, this was not always the case. In the past, several of the larger settlements were independent kingdoms or city-states, each with its own monarch. Over time, through treaties and alliances, the monarchies were peacefully dissolved and replaced by the current system of lords and the [[Valley Council]].

In some settlements, like [[Fountainport]], the title of "King" is still maintained as a traditional honor, held by a ceremonial figurehead. These titular monarchs are respected elders and cultural leaders, but they hold no formal political power.
