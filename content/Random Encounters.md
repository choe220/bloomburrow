## On the Road (More Structured Encounters)

|**d12**|**Encounter**|
|---|---|
|**1**|**The Silent Procession** – A caravan of animalfolk merchants (hedgehog and rabbitfolk) pass by, pulling small wagons covered in ivy. Their faces are solemn, their crates bound in thorny vines. If questioned, they refuse to say what they carry, muttering only that it’s “for the Grove.”|
|**2**|**Traveler in Need** – A frogfolk named Mipple sits beside an overturned cart, his wares (seedcakes, jam, and dried herbs) spilled across the road. He asks for help fixing a wheel but grows nervous if pressed about his destination. If helped, he rewards the party with a **small sachet of calming lavender**.|
|**3**|**Feral Weaving** – A tangle of corrupted thread (_animate vines infused with wild Weaving magic_) thrashes in the road, trying to snare anything that moves. Someone or something lost control of their craft.|
|**4**|**Restless Lights** – At dusk, small, floating **foxfire wisps** drift along the path. Following them leads to an **ancient stone marker**, inscribed with unreadable Weaving symbols.|
|**5**|**Lost Child** – A young squirrelfolk named Brindle emerges from the underbrush, claiming their grandmother vanished after stepping into a shadow. Unnatural footprints lead off the path.|
|**6**|**Spore Cloud Ambush** – A tree with **bracket fungi** suddenly releases a **puff of green spores**, creating a thick haze. Inside the cloud, eerie shapes move—small fungal creatures or something worse.|
|**7**|**Corpseberry Warning** – The road is littered with trampled **Corpseberry bushes**. The berries are stripped away, but **claw marks** and **scorched ground** suggest something unnatural was feeding here.|
|**8**|**Rattled Cart** – A lone badger merchant named Ormo anxiously checks his wagon’s wheels. He’s carrying **a shipment of starwoven thread**, but something has been following him for miles—**shadowy figures** that never quite step into the light.|
|**9**|**Carcass in the Road** – A **partially skeletal stag** lies in the path, its bones unnaturally **clean and arranged in a pattern**. A Weaving ritual? A warning?|
|**10**|**Brambles’ Toll** – A mischievous band of **Spriggan-thorns** (tiny, spiky fey) leap onto the road, demanding an **outrageous toll** (a riddle answered, a song sung, or a shiny trinket).|
|**11**|**Unnatural Stillness** – For a mile, the Brambles are **silent**—no wind, no birds. The party’s shadows seem **slightly delayed** in their movement. A low whispering rises if they remain too long.|
|**12**|**Marked Wanderer** – A molefolk with **glowing stitch-like markings** stumbles toward the party, muttering that they were “woven wrong.” They warn the party of **Bonebound stirring** before collapsing unconscious.|

## Brambles River Travel – Random Encounters

(_Roll 1d12 or choose based on the story’s flow_)

Traveling the **Long River** through the Brambles can be deceptively peaceful. The waterways are old, winding, and lined with ancient roots, tangled undergrowth, and forgotten ruins. The river carries echoes of past conflicts, traces of Weaving magic, and creatures both wondrous and unsettling.

---

### On the River (While Boating or Drifting)

|**d12**|**Encounter**|
|---|---|
|**1**|**The Sunken Weaver** – A **half-submerged boat** drifts slowly, tangled in a thick net of **silken Weaving threads**. The hull bears **strange stitched markings**, and inside are **scraps of an old journal** detailing forbidden Weaving experiments.|
|**2**|**Corpseberry Bloom** – A dense patch of **Corpseberry vines** grows from a **floating log**, pulsing faintly with residual energy. A druid or Weaver might sense **a lost soul trapped** in the berries, whispering faint warnings.|
|**3**|**Fungal Drift** – A **mass of bioluminescent fungi** bobs in the water. If disturbed, it releases **spores** that induce vivid hallucinations of **a battle long past**.|
|**4**|**Foxfire Ripples** – Strange **glowing motes** appear just beneath the river’s surface, **swimming** alongside the boat. If watched too long, a character may see **a flickering face staring back at them**.|
|**5**|**Abandoned Ferry** – A **dilapidated wooden ferry** sits anchored at the river’s edge, its rope frayed but intact. The ferry house is covered in **claw marks**, and something **still lingers inside**.|
|**6**|**Ghostfish Migration** – A school of **ghostly translucent fish** swims beneath the boat. If disturbed, one fish **phases through the hull** and whispers, “Not yet.”|
|**7**|**The River Hag’s Warning** – A **mysterious otterfolk woman**, wrapped in riverweed, floats by on her back. Without opening her eyes, she whispers a cryptic warning: “Turn back before the river turns you.”|
|**8**|**Sunken Ruins** – A half-visible **stone archway** emerges from the water, the remains of an old bridge. Runes along its surface glow faintly, marking it as **a lost Weaver outpost**.|
|**9**|**The Drowned Boatman** – A **skeletal hand** rises suddenly from the water, clutching at the boat’s edge. It holds an **ironwood token**, the kind once used by ferrymen before the Calamity.|
|**10**|**Oar Snatcher** – A **massive waterlogged root** suddenly animates, **grasping** at oars or sails as if trying to **drag the boat under**.|
|**11**|**The Whispering Current** – The river begins **murmuring** in a voice no one quite recognizes, repeating the names of the party in hushed tones.|
|**12**|**Lanterns on the Water** – Small wooden lanterns, each with a **flickering green light**, float down the river in an eerie procession. Following them leads to an **old ritual site**, overgrown and long abandoned.|

## Underground Tunnels – Random Encounters

(_Roll 1d12 or choose based on the story’s flow_)

The tunnels beneath Valley are ancient, winding, and woven with forgotten Weaving magic. They range from tight burrows dug by industrious folk to grand, crumbling passageways left behind by civilizations long past. Some are **natural**, carved by underground rivers, while others are **unnatural**, shaped by hands that no longer walk the surface. The deeper one goes, the more the air **hums** with something old—something waiting.

---

### Tight Burrows & Winding Tunnels (Shallow Depths)

|**d12**|**Encounter**|
|---|---|
|**1**|**Echoing Footsteps** – As the party walks, **their own footsteps** seem to lag behind, just a beat out of sync. Then, **an extra set** joins in.|
|**2**|**Glowing Veins** – The walls of the tunnel **pulse faintly**, lined with veins of **bioluminescent roots**. If disturbed, the light flickers—**and something else flickers in response** deeper in the tunnel.|
|**3**|**Tunnel Collapse!** – A **sudden cave-in** threatens to bury the party! Dodging the falling debris is possible, but they’ll need to **dig themselves out** or find another path.|
|**4**|**Whispering Fissures** – A crack in the tunnel wall **whispers** faint, incomprehensible words. Listening too closely causes the listener to hear **their own voice answering back**.|
|**5**|**Mosslight Motes** – Small glowing **spores** drift lazily through the air, illuminating the tunnels. If inhaled, a character gains **vivid memories of a life that is not their own** for 1 minute.|
|**6**|**Old Weaver Remnants** – The tunnel walls are covered in **fragments of intricate Weaving**, long abandoned. They **still hold power**, but activating them might awaken **something watching from beyond**.|
|**7**|**The Gnawing** – Strange **bite marks** cover the tunnel walls—**perfect circles**, as if something **chewed through** solid stone.|
|**8**|**Burrow Beast** – A **massive mole-like creature**, blind but **with heightened hearing**, crosses paths with the party. It’s not immediately hostile, but **sudden noise** will send it into a frenzy.|
|**9**|**Waterlogged Path** – The tunnel floor is **flooded**, and a slow **current** tugs at anything stepping into it. Something **large** moves beneath the surface.|
|**10**|**Lost Firelight** – A tiny **glowing lantern**, untouched by age, sits on a stone ledge. It casts **no shadow**, and if picked up, **it refuses to go out**—even when submerged in water.|
|**11**|**The Watcher’s Sigil** – An old **marking** is carved into the stone, deep and deliberate. It’s **not in any known language**, and staring too long makes **the viewer feel watched**.|
|**12**|**Sudden Silence** – All sound suddenly **vanishes** for a brief moment—no footsteps, no breathing, no movement. Then, it all comes rushing back.|

### Deep Tunnels & Forgotten Passages (Ancient Depths)

|**d12**|**Encounter**|
|---|---|
|**1**|**The Sleeping Bones** – A **massive skeleton**, partially buried in the tunnel wall, is revealed by crumbling dirt. It’s **too large** to be any known creature.|
|**2**|**The Stitched Ones** – Deep scratches in the walls form **stitched Weaving patterns**, and occasionally, they **shift** when not being watched.|
|**3**|**Hollow Wind** – A cold gust of air blows through the tunnels, carrying a **distant voice** that mutters **a name from the party’s past**.|
|**4**|**Weaver’s Loom** – An old, **abandoned loom** sits in the center of the tunnel. It still holds a piece of **unfinished threadwork**, pulsating faintly.|
|**5**|**The Bonebound Stalker** – Shadows seem **too long** in this part of the tunnel, and every time the party turns a corner, the **echo of slow, dragging footsteps** follows.|
|**6**|**Chasm of Eyes** – A deep ravine splits the tunnel floor, stretching far below. Strange **glowing orbs** flicker from the darkness—**watching**.|
|**7**|**The Forgotten Shrine** – A **half-buried altar**, dedicated to **a lost Weaver god**, sits untouched in the tunnel. If approached, the air **grows thick with unseen threads**.|
|**8**|**Living Stone** – The tunnel walls **shift** slightly when no one is looking, as if breathing. Touching them gives the distinct sensation of **something waking up**.|
|**9**|**Shadow Twin** – A character’s **shadow lingers** unnaturally long, moving **half a second behind them**. Then, for just a moment, it moves **on its own**.|
|**10**|**The Threadbound Guardian** – An old **sentinel**, woven from ancient Weaving magic, stands in the path. It does not move—**until someone speaks**.|
|**11**|**Footsteps in the Dark** – Without warning, the ground trembles with the weight of **many unseen feet** running past. Nothing is there—except for the feeling of something **brushing against their legs**.|
|**12**|**The Weeping Walls** – The tunnel walls **drip with an inky substance**, forming **shifting symbols**. If collected, the ink retains **a faint heartbeat**.|
