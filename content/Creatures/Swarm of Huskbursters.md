# Swarm of Huskbursters

*Large elemental (swarm), Unaligned*

---

**Armor Class** 12
**Hit Points** 38 (7d10)
**Speed** 30 ft.

---

|STR|DEX|CON|INT|WIS|CHA|
|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
|6 (-2)|18 (+4)|11 (+0)|3 (-4)|8 (-1)|6 (-2)|

---

**Damage Resistances** bludgeoning, piercing, slashing
**Condition Immunities** charmed, frightened, grappled, paralyzed, petrified, prone, restrained, stunned
**Senses** darkvision 60 ft., tremorsense 60 ft., passive Perception 9
**Languages** ---
**Challenge** 3

---

***Swarm.*** The swarm of huskbursters can occupy another creature's space and vice versa, and the swarm can move through any opening large enough for a Tiny huskburster. The swarm can't regain hit points or gain temporary hit points.

### Actions

***Multiattack.*** The swarm makes two Swarm of Bites attacks.

***Swarm of Bites.*** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +6 to hit, reach 0 ft., one target. *Hit:* 14 (4d4 + 4) piercing damage, or 9 (2d4 + 4) piercing damage if the swarm is bloodied.

***Infest.*** The swarm loses 1d10 hit points and partially infests the corpse of a Medium or smaller creature within 5 feet. In 24 hours, a Swarm of Huskbursters appears from the infested corpse. Casting *gentle repose* on the corpse ends the infestation early.

---

> Huskbursters aren't Calamity Beasts themselves, but instead Elemental insects that are formed from the remains of fallen Calamity Beasts. They are known to burst forth from the carcasses of Calamity Beasts, hence their name, and descend as a swarm upon any who are nearby. Squirrelfolk necromancers draw power from the bones of Calamity Beasts, and so are intimately familiar with these insects; some necromancers may even be able to command them.
>
> Treasure: Relics
