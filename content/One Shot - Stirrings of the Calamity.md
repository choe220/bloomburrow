### **The Caravan That Never Arrived**

It started with a **simple job**—escort a **merchant caravan** traveling from **Fountainport to Goodhill**. The job wasn’t meant to be **dangerous**. Just a week’s journey along **the Long River**, making sure that the **food, supplies, and rare trade goods** made it safely to the next settlement.

But the caravan **never arrived**.

Instead, the group—**six strangers brought together by chance, reputation, or coin—woke up in the marsh, half-drowned, their cart overturned, the river running red.** The last thing any of them remembered was an evening of **quiet travel**, then… something **rising from the waters.**

The **boat haulers were gone**. The **wagons looted**. And in the mud, scattered among the tracks, were **footprints that didn’t belong to any known animalfolk**—twisted, elongated shapes, as if something had dragged itself from **the depths of the riverbed.**

### **Piecing Together the Truth**

With no supplies, no map, and no idea what happened, the six were **forced to work together**. They followed the **trail through the Marshlands**, where they encountered:

- **Otterfolk Rafts that had seen “lights in the water” the night before**
- **A hidden shrine, half-sunken, filled with carvings of forgotten river spirits**
- **A lone survivor—one of the haulers, driven mad, repeating the same phrase:**  
    _“The river remembers. The river takes back what was stolen.”_

Through trial, combat, and deduction, the party discovered that **their employer, the merchant Aldros Reedwhisker, had lied to them**. The caravan wasn’t carrying simple trade goods—it was **smuggling something ancient**, an artifact stolen from the riverbed **belonging to an old, forgotten force.**

And that force **wanted it back.**

### **From Circumstance to Bond**

In the end, they **barely escaped** with their lives, but the **mystery of the drowned caravan stayed with them**. Someone had **paid a high price to smuggle that artifact**, and someone—or **something**—had been **willing to kill to return it to the water**.

With no payment, no employer, and no easy explanation for what happened, the six were left with **one certainty**—**they had survived together**.

Over the months that followed, they found **more work as a group**, relying on each other’s strengths. Sometimes it was just **mercenary work**, sometimes **retrieval of lost knowledge**, and sometimes, it was **uncovering truths that others wanted buried**.

Now, years later, they are known as **drifters, troubleshooters, and seekers of the unseen.** And though they’ve faced many threats since, the **echo of that night on the Long River still lingers.**

What **was** stolen? Who wanted it gone? And did they ever truly escape it?

The council fears that something, or someone, is interfering with the honored dead.

The council is divided—some want immediate action, while others argue for caution

The **Council Chamber of Three-Tree City** is abuzz with tension. A grand hall woven from **living branches and stone**, its centerpiece is a circular table where **leaders of different animalfolk clans** are engaged in a **heated argument**. The players arrive just as voices rise, overlapping in urgency.

### Trek to the Calamity Graveyard
As the party approaches the **Calamity Graveyard**, the air grows thick with an eerie stillness. The usual hum of insect life is absent, and the wind carries the faint scent of charred wood and something acrid, like burning bone. The land begins to shift beneath their feet—once-firm soil gives way to slick, blackened mud, and twisted roots emerge from the ground like skeletal fingers grasping at the sky.

- **The Bones of Giants**  
    The first sign of the graveyard’s presence is a massive ribcage, half-buried in the muck. Each bone is as tall as a tree, and the marrow within has long since been consumed by time, leaving behind hollowed-out tunnels that smaller creatures once used as dens. Now, they lie abandoned. Something has disturbed them recently—deep, clawed gouges mark the outer walls, as if something had **crawled out** rather than in.
    
- **The Squirrelfolk’s Absence**  
    The deeper the party moves, the more noticeable the **lack of scavengers** becomes. This place should be alive with Squirrelfolk gatherers harvesting **Corpseberries**, the faintly glowing fruits that sprout from the remains of the fallen. But instead, brittle branches droop under the weight of untouched fruit, some of them shriveled and blackened as if something has drained them of vitality. A careful inspection reveals faint footprints, **clusters of hurried steps leading away from the pit**—as if those who had come before fled in terror.
    
- **Whispers on the Wind**  
    A sudden gust of air carries something unnatural—a **whisper**, distant yet distinct, curling through the trees like a dying breath. Those who strain to listen might hear words in an ancient, indecipherable tongue. Those with magical senses or strong intuition will feel it: **this place is waking up**.
    
- **Shifting Tar and Restless Remains**  
    At the heart of the graveyard, the **tar pit** churns sluggishly, forming unnatural ripples. Every so often, **a bone rises to the surface**, slick and stained, before slowly sinking again. Watching closely, the pattern isn’t random—it’s **as if something beneath is moving**, testing the surface, waiting. A successful Arcana or Religion check would reveal that this isn’t natural decay; **someone is disturbing the cycle of death here.**
    
- **A Flicker of Motion**  
    Just as the party takes in the scene, a **figure** moves in the distance—a shadow darting between the bones. At first, it seems like another scavenger, but its movements are **too rigid, too unnatural**. When they draw closer, they see it for what it is: **a Bonebound**, an animated skeleton of a long-dead warrior, its form wreathed in strands of glistening, shadowy thread. It doesn’t attack—**yet**—instead, it **turns its head in a slow, deliberate motion** before slipping back into the darkness.

Shadows stretch unnaturally, coiling like grasping fingers, and then—he steps forward. A Squirrelfolk, though twisted beyond recognition, draped in robes woven from bone-white threads. > He spreads his arms, the air around him growing thick with a choking necrotic energy._
> _"But I will not let their strength go to waste. Rise, my warriors—tear the marrow from their bones."_
